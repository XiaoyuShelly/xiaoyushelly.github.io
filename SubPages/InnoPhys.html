What is Data Physicalization?

Data physicalization is the practice of transforming abstract data into tangible, perceivable physical forms. Unlike digital visualization, it uses real materials, spatial structures, and human-scale construction to create artifacts that simultaneously communicate information and engage the senses.

From data sculptures to tactile infographics, from community-participatory installations to personal data mementos, physicalization is redefining how we relate to and experience data.

How to Use These Cards

The deck follows the design process across four key phases: Context helps you understand the design situation; Structure guides physical form and material choices; Interaction explores audience engagement; and Evaluation provides a systematic assessment framework.

Use the cards in design workshops or individual design sessions — draw relevant dimension cards to spark discussion, trigger ideas, or structure key design decisions.
